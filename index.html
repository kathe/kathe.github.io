Kathe

I’m Kathe; a tinkerer, an Engineer and a Software Programmer from Mumbai, India.

Born in a Marathi speaking Indian Hindu family of the “Somavaunshiya Kshatriya Paathaare” clan of native Bombayites, I bear “Chitaapaavan Konkanastha Brahmin” lineage on my mother’s side.

Tinkering comes naturally to me which was nurtured by my parents by gifting me Meccano sets instead of dumb toy planes and cars during childhood. This was logically carried forward by me acquiring an Engineering Diploma in Fabrication Technology from Fr. Agnel’s Polytechnic (Vashi).

My interest in software programming is the result of being exposed to the craft as a 15 year old at St. John’s High School, Goregaon West (Bombay). The exhilarating experience of creating something tangible out of a bunch of abstractions was so overwhelming that I went on to acquire a Professional Diploma in Network Centered Computing from National Institute of Information Technology (Mumbai) after completing my engineering studies.

I started my career with Larsen & Toubro Ltd., Powai (Mumbai), post which I worked for a string of startup organisations in Mumbai, notable among them being; Tisya (now defunct), Pinstorm, Cleartrip and Ticketvala (which got bought-out by MakeMyTrip).

These days I program software mostly as a hobby and enjoy tinkering around with open source projects usually in the domain of system administration automation for Unix-like operating systems.

Following is the best advice I have received on the topic of committing oneself to open source (software and community) related work.

It was dispensed by Kubilay Kocak <koobs@freebsd.org> to me on 20th January, 2015.

Mayuresh,

I would only suggest that you choose to spend time and energy on things that:

  1. Make you happy,
  2. Keep you inspired, excited & challenged,
  3. Develop you personally, technically and professionally.

If there happen to be overlaps to the above that benefit the FreeBSD Project and it’s community, then that is a bonus. We are willing to support engaged users of the community in any way we can regardless.

Begin with the end in mind, start small, and go from there.

Let me know how you go. If you have any questions, shoot! :)

Koobs

I always keep the above in mind before selecting an open source project to work on.

You can contact me by writing an email to mayuresh@kathe.in and I’ll respond as soon as possible.
